## Prerequisites

To open support tickets and new cases, you need to be registered as an authorized support contact for your company. For more information, reach out to our [Sales team](mailto:sales@prophecy.io).

<Info>
  Users on free versions of Prophecy are not eligible for support. Please refer to self-service
  resources to troubleshoot any issues.
</Info>

## Submit a support ticket

<Steps>

<Step title="Create a new request">

- Open the [Support Portal](https://prophecy.zendesk.com)
- Click **Submit a Ticket**.

</Step>

<Step title="Fill in the form details">
Complete the support request form with the following information.

**Subject**: Enter a short summary of the issue.

**Description**: Provide detailed information about your issue. Include:

- A description of the issue, detailing expected vs. actual behavior.
- Any steps taken so far to troubleshoot the issue.
- The workspace URL where the issue occurred.
- The pipeline, dataset, or other resource involved.

**Urgency**: Select the option that best reflects the impact of the issue.

**CC (optional)**: Add team members to the ticket.

<Warning>Users must exist in Zendesk to be added as CC.</Warning>

**Current Prophecy Version (optional)**: Include your Prophecy version to help diagnose version-specific issues.

**Attachments**: Upload logs, screenshots, or other relevant files (up to 10 MB). For information on what to include, see the [Send logs to Support](/data-analytics/administration/getting-help/prophecy-details) page.

Allowed file types: PDF, PNG, JPEG, GIF, TXT, LOG, HAR, JAR.

</Step>

<Step title="Submit the ticket">
Click **Submit** to create your support ticket.
</Step>
</Steps>

## View my support tickets

<Steps>

<Step title="Open the requests view">

- Open the [Support Portal](https://prophecy.zendesk.com)
- Click **View all tickets**.

</Step>

<Step title="Filter the requests">

You can filter the requests by status, including:

- Any (shows all requests)
- Open
- Awaiting your reply
- Solved

</Step>

<Step title="View additional requests">

In addition to your own requests, you can also view:

- Requests that you are CC'd on
- Organization requests (any ticket submitted by other users in your organization)

</Step>
</Steps>

## Case lifecycle

The following stages are used to track support ticket progress.

- **New**: Ticket has been submitted.
- **In Progress**: The Support team is actively investigating.
- **Awaiting Customer**: You need to provide additional information to Prophecy.
- **Escalated**: Engineering or other teams engaged if necessary.
- **Resolved**: Prophecy has provided a fix, workaround, or explanation.
- **Closed**: The case has been completed.

## Case severity

Prophecy uses severity levels to prioritize issues based on business impact.

| Level                      | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                    | Initial Response | Coverage                  |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------- | ------------------------- |
| **Severity 1**<br />Urgent | Production use is blocked/broken without a workaround.<ul><li>Workspace unavailable</li><li>Production pipelines failing</li></ul>                                                                                             | < 1 hour         | 24/7 every day            |
| **Severity 2**<br />High   | Major functionality is impaired. System is functional but in a degraded or restricted state. <ul><li>Significant performance degradation</li><li>Repeated failures</li><li>Blocking issues with possible workarounds</li></ul> | < 4 hours        | 24/7 every day            |
| **Severity 3**<br />Medium | Non-critical functionality is impacted or a development-only issue occurs. <ul><li>Minor errors</li><li>UI or workflow issues</li><li>Questions about expected behavior</li></ul>                                              | < 1 business day | 9 AM - 6 PM business days |
| **Severity 4**<br />Low    | General questions or feature requests with no production impact.<ul><li>How-to questions</li><li>Documentation clarifications</li><li>Enhancement ideas</li></ul>                                                              | < 1 business day | 9 AM - 6 PM business days |

If a ticket requires additional attention, you may escalate by:

- Updating the ticket with new details.
- Contacting your customer success manager.
